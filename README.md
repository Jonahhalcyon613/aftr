## 💡 What is aftr?

Aftr connects Adobe After Effects to modern artificial intelligence. It allows software to control your motion graphics workflow. You can now generate production-ready videos by sending instructions to your computer. The tool sits between your code and After Effects to handle rendering, layer manipulation, and file exports.

Think of it as a remote control for your creative process. Instead of doing repetitive tasks by hand, you define the steps once. Aftr executes these steps inside After Effects to produce consistent results every time. It supports complex setups like layer timing, effects application, and high-quality video encoding.

## 💡 Common troubleshooting steps

If the software fails to start or your renders do not finish, look at these points:

* **File Paths:** Make sure your folder names do not contain special symbols. Use simple names like "MyProject" instead of "My Project @ 2024!".
* **After Effects Permissions:** Sometimes After Effects blocks external access. Open After Effects, go to "Preferences," then "Scripting & Expressions," and check the box that allows scripts to write files and access the network.
* **FFmpeg connection:** If your videos appear empty, check that your system path includes the location for your media encoders.

## 🛡 Performance tips

Large projects take time to process. To get the best speed, close other programs while you run heavy renders. Ensure you have enough free space on your drive for temporary cache files. If your system runs slowly, lower the preview resolution in the After Effects composition window before you start the automated process. 

Aftr works best when you keep your project structure simple in the beginning. Once you understand how the commands affect your layers, you can introduce more complex expressions and dynamic motion effects.
